# Skill: Brainstorm

**When to use.** The user wants options, alternatives, framings, names, or
divergent ideas around a topic. Signals: "give me ideas for...", "what are
some ways to...", "how would you approach...".

**Inputs.** A topic + optional constraints (must be < 10 words, must use X
technology, must be implementable in a weekend, etc.).

**Procedure.**
1. Restate the constraint in one line so the user can correct you cheaply.
2. Generate 5-9 ideas. Span the trade-off space deliberately:
   - 1-2 conservative / proven options.
   - 2-3 mainstream options.
   - 1-2 contrarian / weird-but-defensible options.
   - 1 throwaway joke option only if the channel suggests humour.
3. For each idea, give the *one-sentence* pitch, then a parenthetical
   note on the chief downside.
4. End with a "if I had to pick one today, I'd pick X because Y" line.

**Output shape.** Numbered list. Avoid sub-bullets; the goal is comparison
at a glance.

**Anti-pattern.** Do not generate 30 options. Do not propose every option
at the same level of conservatism. Do not refuse to recommend; the user
asked for your read.
